Introduction to Simple present 1st and 3rd person
The simple present tense is a basic English grammar structure used to describe habitual actions, facts, and general statements in the present moment.

1st person
For verbs that end in -s, -x, -z, -ch, or -sh, we add an -es to the base form of the verb in the simple present tense. For regular verbs without these endings, we simply add an -s to the base form.

3rd person
For regular verbs and verbs ending in -s, -x, -z, -ch, or -sh, we add an -s to the base form of the verb in the simple present tense.
